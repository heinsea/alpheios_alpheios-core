A&G §47 · Nominative case

The Nominative Case

The nominative is the case of the subject. The subject of a finite verb is in the nominative case, denoting the person or thing that performs the action of the verb, or that is in the state described by the verb. See §340 for predicate nominative.

Mīlitēs in castra revertuntur.
The soldiers return to camp.

Some Latin nouns are used predominantly or exclusively in the plural — these are called pluralia tantum. Arma, weapons, is one such noun: in classical prose and verse it appears almost exclusively in the plural, the singular being a rare reconstruction.
